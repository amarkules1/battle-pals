# Battle Pals: Plot and World Design

This document details the plot, setting, progression, and mechanics for the expanded version of Battle Pals.

---

## 🧑‍🔬 Main Character

The player character (name and gender selectable) is a university student and biology researcher who has recently relocated from **Liberty City** (a bustling, free city-state) to **Paltopia** (a vast and highly regulated country). 

* **The Mission**: The player is commissioned by their university's biology department to study Paltopia's native wildlife and compile the first comprehensive **Paldex** (Research Database).
* **The Tension**: In Liberty City, Battle Pals do not exist natively; they are treated as mythical creatures or foreign legends. In Paltopia, Pals are abundant but tightly controlled by the state. As a foreign researcher, the player's presence is tolerated only under heavy surveillance.

---

## 🇨🇳 Setting: Paltopia vs. Liberty City

The world's political and social structure draws thematic inspiration from the relationship between Hong Kong (Liberty City) and Mainland China (Paltopia):

1. **Liberty City**: A highly advanced, cosmopolitan, and democratic city-state. While technologically superior, it has no native Pals, meaning its citizens have never learned to live or battle alongside them.
2. **Paltopia**: A sprawling country divided into **9 Precincts**, characterized by beautiful landscapes and an abundance of wild Pals. Paltopia is governed by an authoritarian regime that controls the population by strictly regulating access to Pals.
   * **The State Narrative**: The government claims that Pals are extremely dangerous beasts and that citizens must be "protected" from them. Owning or interacting with Pals without state clearance is strictly illegal.
   * **The Reality**: The regime uses trained Pals as tools of policing, surveillance, and labor, maintaining a monopoly on their power to keep the population compliant.

## 📈 Progression & Precinct Gates

Travel between precincts is heavily restricted. Each boundary is secured by a **Precinct Gate** (North, South, East, or West checkpoints).
* **Clearance Licenses**: To pass a gate, the player must defeat the precinct's ruling bureaucrat in a formal, high-stakes battle to prove their "biological research competence" and force them to sign a travel waiver.
* **Fast Travel**: Defeating a bureaucrat unlocks fast-travel nodes at the gates of that precinct, allowing quick backtracking to previous zones.

## 🧪 Core Gameplay Mechanics

### 1. The Post-Combat Capture Loop
* **Capture Cubes**: Unlike traditional monster-battlers where capturing happens during combat, Paltopian law restricts the use of Capture Cubes. The player's Pal must **defeat the wild Pal first** to render it incapacitated.
* **The Catch Phase**: Once a wild Pal faints, a short transition phase begins where the player can throw a Capture Cube. Success rates depend on:
  * The quality of the Cube used.
  * The species' research level (Paldex completion).
  * How clean the victory was (e.g. defeating it quickly or with status debuffs).
* **Faint Restriction**: If the player's last Pal faints in the same round the wild Pal is defeated (e.g., through status damage, recoil, or a double-faint attack), the battle ends in defeat immediately, and the player loses the opportunity to capture the Pal.

### 2. Party Size & The 10-Pal Squad
* **Roster Flexibility**: The player can carry up to **10 Pals** in their active party, with battles conducted in a standard 1v1 format. 
* **Tactical Selection**: The 10-slot party functions as a highly flexible roster, allowing the player to carry diverse elemental typings and specimens ready for action. There are no passive combat buffs or stat bonuses provided by reserve Pals, keeping the focus entirely on tactical matchup switching.

### 3. Biology Research (The Paldex Loop)
* Documenting a species is a multi-step process. Players earn **Research Points (RP)** by:
  1. Observing a Pal in the wild (unlocks silhouette).
  2. Defeating it in battle (unlocks stats and typing).
  3. Capturing it (unlocks learnsets and traits).
  4. Using it in battle (unlocks deep lore and evolution details).
* **Research Milestones**: Accumulating RP allows the player to upgrade their Capture Cubes, gain access to exclusive evolutionary items, or purchase advanced training manuals from local resistance labs.

### 4. Precinct Labs & The Underground Network
* Government labs are scattered throughout the precincts. Nominally used for state research, the player can hack into these labs (aided by a rebel faction) to:
  * Heal their party.
  * Access the storage network (the **Pal Storage System**).
  * Black-market shopping for Capture Cubes, supplies, and unregistered tech.
